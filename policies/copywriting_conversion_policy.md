# Copywriting Conversion Policy

## Purpose

`copywriting_conversion_readiness` exists to review commercial clarity, trust and conversion hygiene in a governed Atlas-native way.

The goal is not to generate prettier hype. The goal is to check whether a page explains:

- what the product is
- who it is for
- what problem it solves
- why it matters
- what the next step is
- what objections remain unanswered

## Core rules

- advisory only
- no guaranteed sales, profitability or conversion claims
- no invented proof, metrics or customer evidence
- no aggressive sales pressure as a substitute for clarity
- no visual redesign inside this layer
- no automatic edits to derived projects

## What the layer should flag

- unclear hero message
- missing audience or problem framing
- weak or misleading CTAs
- forms that do not explain what happens next
- privacy or consent ambiguity
- generic AI-style filler
- unsupported commercial claims
- instant-diagnosis promises without real backend support

## Output posture

The layer should return:

- readiness state
- clarity score
- conversion score
- trust score
- tone consistency score
- warnings and risks
- missing inputs
- recommended copy changes
- must-not-claim list

## Safety boundary

This layer may recommend better copy structure and clearer microcopy, but it must not:

- claim business outcomes
- impersonate legal/privacy review
- fake evidence
- imply backend capabilities that do not exist
